PART 1: The state of agentic AI for BIM (2024–2026)
Multi-agent architectures have overtaken single-model approaches
The dominant paradigm in BIM-AI research has shifted decisively to multi-agent LLM systems with role-based decomposition. Of the four major frameworks evaluated, Microsoft AutoGen has the strongest documented BIM application — the "AutoGenRevit" prototype, published in Automation in Construction (2025), achieved 84.62% execution success and 76.92% accuracy for natural-language-driven design coordination in Revit. PART 2: Embodied carbon is becoming the defining metric for construction
EDGE V3 shifts from embodied energy to embodied carbon
EDGE (Excellence in Design for Greater Efficiencies), created by IFC/World Bank Group EDGE +3 in 2014, has grown to cover 130+ million square meters EDGE Buildings of certified floor space across 129+ countries, saving over 1.3 million tons of CO2 annually. International Finance Corporation Its three certification tiers — EDGE Certified (≥20% savings), EDGE EDGE Advanced (≥40% energy savings), and EDGE Zero Carbon (100% renewable energy) U.S. Green Building CouncilEDGE Buildings — apply across homes, offices, hospitals, retail, education, and hospitality. EDGE
The critical V3 update shifts EDGE's material assessment from embodied energy (MJ) to embodied carbon (GWP in kgCO2eq), aligned with EN 15804+A2:2019. The methodology uses a cradle-to-gate scope covering EDGE Buildings stages A1 (raw materials), A2 (transport to manufacturing), and A3 (manufacturing). EDGE Buildings EDGE evaluates embodied carbon across 11 Material Efficiency Measures covering floors, walls, roofs, windows, and insulation, each comparing a base case with improved design choices.
EDGE's calculation engine uses a monthly quasi-steady-state method based on CEN/ISO 13790 standards, edgebuildings validated against dynamic simulation software (eQuest) with ~5% variation. EDGE Buildings For each location, the EDGE App contains embedded climate data including temperature, humidity, solar radiation, grid CO2 intensity, and energy costs. edgebuildings Four baseline types exist: country-customized, city-customized, global EDGE, and ASHRAE 90.1-2016. edgebuildings
A critical limitation for platform integration: the EDGE App has no public API and no direct BIM integration. Users must manually input building parameters. However, the app is free and web-based (app.edgebuildings.com), EDGE and BIM models can theoretically extract the required data for EDGE input — creating a clear automation opportunity.

Thai BOQ practice lacks a national standard, creating both challenge and opportunity
Thailand does not have a published national Standard Method of Measurement like the UK's NRM or Singapore's frameworks. Thai quantity surveyors reference international standards (POMI, FIDIC, CSI MasterFormat) rather than a Thai-specific system. BOQ preparation typically follows five sections: earthworks, structural works, architecture, systems (MEP), and other. No known Thai government database systematically links material quantities to emission factors.
This absence of standardization is paradoxically an opportunity: a platform that defines the mapping between Thai BOQ items and carbon emission factors could establish the de facto national standard. Building a baseline model requires layered data integration
Best practice for carbon baseline creation follows a clear methodology: prepare detailed BOQ with material quantities → map BOQ items to emission factor databases using standardized classification codes → apply the equation Embodied Carbon = Σ(Material Quantity × Emission Factor) → use primary data (supplier EPDs) where available and secondary data (TGO CFP, ICE database, ecoinvent) where not → apply coverage adjustment factors for unquantified items → report in kgCO2e/m² of GFA. Tunley Environmental
